Universal Quantum Algorithm Conceptual Framework

Your original N-slits / coherent branches diagram is reframed as a broader algorithm-reading model. It is not only a QFT diagram; it is a reusable way to read quantum workflows from representation to circuit, noise, measurement, and interpretation.

Core abstraction

Coherent branches are the common reading lens.

In phase algorithms, coherent branches reveal phase structure. In variational algorithms, coherent branches become candidate physical states or candidate solutions shaped by a Hamiltonian, ansatz, measurement, and classical feedback loop.

Phenomenon→Branches / Basis→Geometry→Hamiltonian / Unitary→Circuit→Readout→Diagnose→Interpret

Your Source Models

These are the original assets behind the lab: the conceptual bridge, skill scaffold, and Phase 2 implementation workflow.

Conceptual Quantum Algorithm Framework

The N-slits / coherent branches model reframed as a universal algorithm-reading lens.

Used to explain how physical intuition maps to Hilbert geometry, circuits, measurement, noise, and post-processing.

Quantum Thinking Skill Set

The production scaffold that turns understanding into practical modeling and circuit decisions.

Used as a workshop checklist: problem abstraction, encoding, sanity checks, oracle/mixer thinking, cost analysis, and explanation.

Phase 2 Workflow

The application workflow: puzzle → QUBO → QAOA → probabilities → apply plan.

Shows visible decision variables and hidden parity helper bits used in the exact QUBO.

Educational Bridge

Double-slit → superposition → entanglement as a learning bridge, not as a replacement for the optimization model.

Used to ground amplitude, phase, interference, and the distinction between superposition and entanglement.
